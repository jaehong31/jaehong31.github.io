I am an Assistant Professor at the College of Computing and Data Science (CCDS) at Nanyang Technological University (NTU) Singapore . Prior to joining NTU, I was a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the MURGe-Lab ( UNC-NLP Group ) at the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill , working with Prof. Mohit Bansal . I received my Ph.D. degree from Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) , advised by Prof. Sung Ju Hwang . I received my B.S. and M.S. degrees from Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST) .

My research broadly focuses on building AI systems that can live and learn in the real world. I develop agents that perceive and reason over rich multimodal signals, interact with complex environments, and continually evolve to be reliable, adaptive, and trustworthy over time, including research on:

Multimodal Understanding
Embodied AI, Many-Modal Reasoning, Vision-Language-Action Reasoning, Long Video Understanding, Small and Large Multimodal Models, Multimodal Evaluation Benchmarks, etc.
Self-Evolving AI
Lifelong Learning, Open-Ended Learning, Multimodal Memory Systems, Model Editing and Skill Updating, Generative Policy Planning, Test-Time Training, etc.
Video Generation
Text/Image-to-Video Models, World Modeling, Multiview Video Synthesis, Long-Range Video Prediction, Physics-Informed Video Generation, etc.
Trustworthy AI
Hallucination Detection and Correction, Safety-Aware Image and Video Generation, Safety-Aware Planning, Explainable and Verifiable Reasoning, AIGC Safety and Quality Evaluation, etc.
Multi-Agent System
Coding Agents, Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning, Role-Specialized Agent and AI Persona, Communication-Efficient Agent Collaboration, Multi-Agent Simulation Environments, etc.
